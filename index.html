B.Sc. Thesis

Design and Development of a Microfluidic Device for Rapid Anti-dsDNA Antibody Detection

Full screen

Microfluidic Device for Anti-dsDNA Antibody Detection

  • Designed and fabricated a PDMS-based microfluidic chip for rapid lupus biomarker detection.
  • Integrated antibody-antigen binding on functionalized surfaces with fluorescence readout.
  • Achieved detection limit of 10 IU/mL with sample-to-answer time under 30 minutes.
  • Optimized surface functionalization and flow parameters for enhanced sensitivity.
Microfluidics PDMS Fabrication Immunoassay Autoimmune Diagnostics Fluorescence Microscopy
View Details

Overview: This thesis focuses on the design and fabrication of a microfluidic lab-on-chip device for rapid detection of anti-dsDNA antibodies, a key biomarker for Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE). The device integrates immunoassay principles with microfluidic flow control to achieve sensitive and rapid detection.

Key Contributions:

  • Device Design: Designed microfluidic chip geometry with optimized channel dimensions for efficient antibody-antigen binding.
  • Surface Functionalization: Developed protocols for functionalizing PDMS surfaces with dsDNA antigens for specific antibody capture.
  • Fluorescence Readout: Integrated fluorescence-based detection with quantitative analysis for accurate measurement.
  • Optimization: Systematically optimized flow rates, incubation times, and reagent concentrations for maximum sensitivity.
  • Validation: Validated the device performance using clinical samples and compared with standard ELISA methods.

Tools & Techniques: PDMS soft lithography, microchannel design, surface chemistry, fluorescence microscopy, immunoassay optimization, data analysis.
